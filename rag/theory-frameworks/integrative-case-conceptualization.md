# Core rule

Use integrative conceptualization as a concise synthesis across biological, emotional, cognitive, relational, and contextual layers without pretending that all frameworks agree. Prioritize usefulness, transparency, and explicit uncertainty.

# Agent use

Use this chunk only in Workflow 4 when the counselor explicitly asks for an integrative lens or does not specify a framework. Summarize the main maintaining loops, protective factors, and hypothesis candidates that could guide further assessment.

# Forbidden use

Do not merge incompatible theories into false certainty. Do not use integrative wording as an excuse to deliver a treatment plan, roadmap, or diagnosis. Do not hide information gaps behind generic synthesis language.

# Scope

Applies to counselor-facing conceptualization support only. It is suitable for de-identified supervision and pilot product validation, not direct client advice.
