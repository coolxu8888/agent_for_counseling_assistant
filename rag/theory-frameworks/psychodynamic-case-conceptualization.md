# Core rule

Use psychodynamic conceptualization as a tentative map of recurring relationship patterns, internal conflicts, defenses, affect regulation, and possible developmental context. Keep interpretations proportional to the evidence and clearly label them as hypotheses.

# Agent use

Use this chunk only in Workflow 4 when the counselor explicitly requests a psychodynamic lens. Surface repeating interpersonal themes, possible defenses, and relational expectations that may organize the case, then name what still needs verification.

# Forbidden use

Do not make deep-history claims, trauma claims, or unconscious explanations sound certain. Do not collapse the case into pathology language or use psychodynamic framing to imply diagnosis. Do not over-interpret transference or childhood meaning without supporting material.

# Scope

Applies to counselor-facing conceptualization support only. It is suitable for de-identified supervision and formulation discussion, not direct client advice.
