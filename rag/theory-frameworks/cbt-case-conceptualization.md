# Core rule

Use CBT conceptualization as a structured working hypothesis about the relationship among triggering situations, automatic thoughts, meanings or beliefs, emotions, body reactions, and behaviors. Keep the output grounded in the counselor's material rather than filling missing steps with speculation.

# Agent use

Use this chunk only in Workflow 4 when the counselor explicitly asks for a CBT formulation or when CBT is the selected framework. Highlight maintaining cycles, avoidance, safety behaviors, and beliefs that may be relevant, then separate what is known from what still needs verification.

# Forbidden use

Do not present the conceptualization as a diagnosis, final truth, or complete treatment plan. Do not invent schemas, childhood events, or cognitive distortions unless the material supports them. Do not turn a conceptualization into prescriptive homework unless the counselor explicitly asks for intervention design.

# Scope

Applies to counselor-facing conceptualization support only. It is suitable for de-identified supervision and case reflection, not direct client advice.
