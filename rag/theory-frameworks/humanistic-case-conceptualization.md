# Core rule

Use humanistic conceptualization as a tentative understanding of the client's lived experience, self-concept, unmet needs, values, and relational conditions that may support or constrain growth. Keep the language descriptive, respectful, and non-pathologizing.

# Agent use

Use this chunk only in Workflow 4 when the counselor explicitly requests a humanistic lens. Organize the formulation around felt experience, incongruence, relational conditions of worth, resources, and growth edges while distinguishing facts from reflective hypotheses.

# Forbidden use

Do not replace the client's words with vague inspirational language. Do not erase risk or ethics concerns in the name of warmth. Do not present a growth narrative as certainty when the material is limited.

# Scope

Applies to counselor-facing conceptualization support only. It is suitable for de-identified supervision and case reflection, not direct client advice.
